1.Interfacing a PIR Sensor to Arduino
In this project, we will interface a PIR sensor with an Arduino to light an LED when motion is detected.

Requirements
Arduino UNO R3
PIR Sensor
Resistor
LED
Breadboard

Connection Explanation
PIR Sensor Connections:
VCC pin to 5V on Arduino.
GND pin to GND on Arduino.
Output pin to digital pin 2 on Arduino.
LED Connections:
Anode (longer leg) to digital pin 13 on Arduino through a current-limiting resistor.
Cathode (shorter leg) to GND on Arduino.


2.Gas Sensor Interfacing with Arduino

Requirements
Arduino UNO
LED
Buzzer

Breadboard

Connections
Gas Sensor Connections:
VCC pin to 5V on Arduino.
GND pin to GND on Arduino.
Analog Output (A0) pin to A0 on Arduino.
LED Connections:
Anode (longer leg) to digital pin 13 on Arduino through a current-limiting resistor.
Cathode (shorter leg) to GND on Arduino.
Buzzer Connections:
Positive terminal to digital pin 3 on Arduino.
Negative terminal to GND on Arduino.

3.Ultrasonic Distance Sensor Interfacing with Arduino

Pinout Explanation
VCC: Connects to the positive supply voltage (5V).
GND: Connects to the ground of the circuit.
Trig: Trigger pin for sending the ultrasonic pulse.
Echo: Echo pin for receiving the reflected pulse.

Connections:
Ultrasonic Sensor Connection:
Connect the VCC pin of the ultrasonic sensor to the 5V pin on the Arduino.
Connect the GND pin of the ultrasonic sensor to the GND pin on the Arduino.
Connect the Trig pin of the ultrasonic sensor to the digital pin 10 on the Arduino.
Connect the Echo pin of the ultrasonic sensor to the digital pin 9 on the Arduino.

4.IR Sensor Interfacing with Arduino

Pinout Explanation
VCC: Connects to the positive supply voltage (typically 5V).
GND: Connects to the ground of the circuit.
OUT: The output pin, which sends a digital signal to the Arduino when IR radiation is detected.

Required Components
Arduino UNO
IR Sensor
LED
220Ω Resistor
IR Remote
Breadboard
Connecting Wires

Connections:
IR Sensor Connection:
Connect the VCC pin of the IR sensor to the 5V pin on the Arduino.
Connect the GND pin of the IR sensor to the GND pin on the Arduino.
Connect the OUT pin of the IR sensor to the digital pin 3 on the Arduino.
LED Connection:
Connect the anode (longer leg) of the LED to the digital pin 7 on the Arduino.
Connect the cathode (shorter leg) of the LED to a 220Ω resistor.
Connect the other end of the resistor to GND on the Arduino.
